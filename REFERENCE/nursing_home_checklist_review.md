# Nursing Home Checklist Review — CQC Draft Assessment Framework (ASC v9)

**Date:** 2026-07-24  
**Scope:** All 84 Nursing Home Core checklist items, with corrections from migrations 20260719 and 20260720 applied  
**Framework:** CQC Adult Social Care Assessment Framework — Draft v9 (3,911 lines, read in full)  
**Method:** Each item assessed against the CQC Good-level rating characteristics for the relevant KLOE; nursing-home-specific context (registered nurses on-site, clinical risk, medicines management) applied throughout

**Assessment key:**  
✅ Keep — accurately reflects CQC Good-level language and is appropriately worded  
✏️ Revise — item is directionally correct but wording should be updated  
❌ Remove — not well-grounded in the framework for this service type

## Gaps — Items to Add

The following topics are explicitly addressed in the CQC Good-level rating characteristics or listed as topic areas within a KLOE, but have no corresponding checklist item for Nursing Homes. Suggested new items are calibrated to the Good rating level (the baseline for a compliant, inspectable service).

---

### SAFE — Safety Culture

**GAP-SC-01 — Closed culture awareness**

*CQC basis:* Explicit topic area. CQC Good: "Staff and leaders understand what constitutes a closed culture and the risks to people, including organisational abuse. There are systems and processes in place to identify concerns and prevent closed cultures from developing, and appropriate action is taken when needed."

> **Suggested item:** Staff and leaders can demonstrate understanding of the risks of a closed culture; monitoring arrangements (e.g. unannounced observations, anonymous feedback channels, quality walks) are in place to identify and prevent closed cultures from developing

---

### SAFE — Managing Risks

**GAP-MR-01 — Clinical deterioration recognition and escalation**

*CQC basis:* "Deterioration, emergencies and clinical risks are anticipated where possible and managed to reduce the potential for harm." (CQC Good, Managing Risks)

> **Suggested item:** A recognised clinical deterioration tool (e.g. NEWS2 or equivalent) is used consistently to identify and escalate deteriorating residents; staff are trained in its use and escalation pathways (including out-of-hours contact with GP, 111 or 999) are documented

**GAP-MR-02 — Positive risk-taking**

*CQC basis:* Explicit topic area. CQC Good: "There is a balanced and proportionate approach to risk. People's rights and choices about their care and their lives are respected. People are supported to take carefully managed risks to live fulfilling lives."

> **Suggested item:** Care plans document where residents have chosen to take positive risks (e.g. walking unsupported, dietary choices); these are recorded with an appropriate risk assessment and the resident's informed agreement, reflecting a least-restrictive approach

**GAP-MR-03 — Restrictive practices review**

*CQC basis:* Explicit topic area. CQC Good: "The service has a process to ensure that any restrictions on people's freedom, choice and control are necessary, proportionate and safe. This particularly includes where people lack mental capacity. Restraint is only ever used as a last resort."

> **Suggested item:** All restrictive practices (including bedrails, lap belts, door alarms, restricted access to areas) are individually assessed, documented, reviewed regularly with the resident or their representative, and reduced where possible; a register of restrictions is maintained

---

### SAFE — Safe Systems, Pathways and Transitions

**GAP-SP-01 — Delegation of clinical/healthcare activities**

*CQC basis:* Explicit topic area. CQC Good: "The service has appropriate systems and processes to manage its responsibilities in relation to delegated healthcare activities safely, in line with good practice."

> **Suggested item:** Where registered nurses delegate clinical tasks to healthcare assistants (e.g. blood glucose monitoring, catheter care, wound dressing under supervision), a formal delegation framework with competency assessment records is in place and operated in line with NMC and RCN delegation guidance

---

### SAFE — Safeguarding

**GAP-SG-01 — Deprivation of Liberty Safeguards (DoLS)**

*CQC basis:* Explicit topic area under Safeguarding. CQC Good: "People are deprived of their liberty lawfully. Any potential deprivation of liberty is recognised promptly and appropriate authorisation is sought. Where applicable, the Deprivation of Liberty Safeguards (DoLS) are used appropriately and only when it is in the best interests of the person. The service regularly reviews care arrangements and makes adjustments to ensure any DoLS conditions are met."

> **Suggested item:** DoLS applications are made promptly when a deprivation of liberty is identified; a DoLS register is maintained showing current authorisations, conditions and review dates; DoLS conditions are incorporated into care plans and reviewed regularly

**GAP-SG-02 — Sexual safety and empowerment**

*CQC basis:* Explicit topic area under Safeguarding. CQC Good: "People are empowered to develop and maintain safe intimate relationships, where they choose to, in accordance with their rights."

### SAFE — Safe Staffing

**GAP-SS-01 — Staff fatigue management**

*CQC basis:* CQC Good: "Actions are taken to protect staff from fatigue, and leaders understand its impact on the safety of those who use services."

> **Suggested item:** Rotas are managed to minimise excessive consecutive shifts; long-shift patterns are monitored; managers can demonstrate awareness of fatigue risks and how these are mitigated (e.g. limits on hours worked, rest break arrangements for night staff)

**GAP-SS-02 — Induction programme**

*CQC basis:* CQC Good: "There are induction, supervision and appraisal processes to support staff to develop and improve services (including professional revalidation where needed)."

> **Suggested item:** A structured induction programme is in place for all new staff, including agency staff, covering safeguarding, medicines, moving and handling, IPC, and the specific needs of residents; completion is recorded before staff work unsupervised

### SAFE — Medicines and Treatments

**GAP-MT-01 — PRN medicines protocols**

*CQC basis:* Explicit topic area. CQC Good: "The administration of PRN medicines (medicines taken when required) is guided by clear protocols, and there are timely reviews."

> **Suggested item:** PRN medicines (including pain relief, anxiolytics and laxatives) have person-specific protocols documenting indication, dose, frequency, maximum dose and review triggers; MAR charts record the reason for each PRN administration

**GAP-MT-02 — Covert medicines administration**

*CQC basis:* Explicit topic area. CQC Good: "People receiving their medicines covertly are supported in line with the Mental Capacity Act 2005. Staff fully understand any risk associated with mixing medicines with different foods."

> **Suggested item:** Where medicines are administered covertly, a valid MCA best interests decision is documented, a pharmacist or prescriber has confirmed the medicine can be safely disguised in food or drink, and covert administration is reviewed regularly; staff understand the legal requirements

**GAP-MT-03 — Antimicrobial stewardship**

*CQC basis:* Explicit topic area in the CQC Safe Medicines KLOE scope. Particularly relevant for nursing homes given infection risk, antibiotic resistance and prescribing relationships with GPs and infection control teams.

> **Suggested item:** The service supports antimicrobial stewardship by ensuring antibiotic prescribing is reviewed, completing antibiotic audit data (e.g. via TARGET Antibiotics), reporting HCAI data as required, and ensuring nursing staff are trained to recognise signs of infection and escalate to prescribers appropriately

**GAP-MT-04 — Self-medication and medicines independence**

*CQC basis:* Explicit topic area. CQC Good: "People and their representatives are involved in assessments, reviews and decisions, including the level of support needed and self-medication."

> **Suggested item:** Residents who wish to and are assessed as able to self-administer their medicines are supported to do so; a self-medication assessment is completed and reviewed regularly; medicines support is proportionate to each person's needs

**GAP-CT-01 — IMCA (Independent Mental Capacity Advocate)**
