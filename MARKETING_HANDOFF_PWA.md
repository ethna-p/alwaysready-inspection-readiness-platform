## What was built

AlwaysReady is now a Progressive Web App (PWA). Users can install it directly from their browser — it appears as an icon on their home screen and opens full-screen with no browser chrome, exactly like a native app. No App Store, no download.

This was deployed to the platform on 20 July 2026 (commit `76e73ef`).

### Feature bullets

- Staff assigned tasks can check and update them on the go, between visits, or on shift
- Managers get the full desktop view; staff see exactly what they need

### How to install

**On iPhone (Safari only)**
Open AlwaysReady in Safari, tap the share icon at the bottom of the screen, then choose *Add to Home Screen*. You'll find the AlwaysReady icon on your home screen ready to go.

**On Android or desktop (Chrome)**
Chrome will show an *Install* prompt automatically when you visit the platform. Tap it, confirm, and AlwaysReady appears on your home screen or taskbar like any other app.

### Closing line (optional)
No IT department needed. No App Store submission. Just a platform your whole team can actually use.

## Important technical notes

- **iPhone requires Safari.** The install prompt does not appear in Chrome on iOS — only Safari supports it. This is an Apple restriction, not an AlwaysReady one. The copy above already reflects this, but make sure it's clear in any how-to guide.
- **Android and desktop Chrome** show the install prompt automatically — no action needed from the user other than accepting it.
- **The platform URL** is the same as always — there is no separate app URL to direct users to.

## What not to say

- Don't use the term "PWA" or "Progressive Web App" in user-facing copy — it means nothing to care managers and staff.
- Don't say it works offline — it requires an internet connection to load compliance data. The offline page shown without a connection makes this clear, but don't make it a selling point.
- Don't say it's available on the App Store or Google Play — it isn't, and doesn't need to be.

## Framework accuracy note (for any marketing copy about platform capabilities)

AlwaysReady is built on the **CQC Adult Social Care sector-differentiated assessment framework (draft)**. This is distinct from the Single Assessment Framework (SAF), which is CQC's cross-sector framework for NHS and other providers.

Key points for accurate marketing copy:

- The platform covers **5 key questions**: Safe, Effective, Caring, Responsive, Well-Led
- These key questions underpin **24 Key Lines of Enquiry (KLOEs)** — these are sector-specific to adult social care
- The framework is currently in **draft**; CQC will publish the final version in **Autumn 2026**
- The 5 key questions and 'I' statements are stable and will not change
- The platform includes the full Outstanding / Good / Requires Improvement / Inadequate rating characteristics for each KLOE, taken verbatim from the CQC draft

**Do not** refer to this as the "Single Assessment Framework", "SAF", or imply that the platform follows the same framework used by NHS trusts or other non-ASC providers. The sector-differentiated framework is specific to adult social care, which is exactly what AlwaysReady serves.
